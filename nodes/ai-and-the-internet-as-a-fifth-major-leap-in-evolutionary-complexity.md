# AI and the Internet as a Fifth Major Leap in Evolutionary Complexity

Inside the transition, without a map

---

> Humanity may be undergoing a fifth major transition in evolutionary complexity — comparable to the emergence of life or language — as AI and digital networks create a new plane of existence, and we lack the conceptual clarity to navigate it wisely.

We may be living through a fifth joint point in the history of evolutionary complexity — a transition as significant as the emergence of life, nervous systems, or propositional language. The tree of knowledge framework identifies a recurring pattern: whenever a new kind of information-processing and communication network emerges, it opens up a qualitatively new plane of existence. Cells communicating with cells gave rise to multicellular life. Nervous systems coordinating animals gave rise to behavioral complexity. Propositional language connecting humans gave rise to the culture-person plane. Now, artificial intelligence, the internet, large language models, and global digital infrastructure represent a fourth such network — one that is generating a potentially new complex adaptive plane of existence at the global level. This is not merely technological change; it is a structural transition in the architecture of how information is processed and communicated at civilizational scale. What makes this moment both extraordinary and dangerous is that we are inside the transition, without the conceptual clarity or value orientation needed to navigate it wisely. The question UTOK poses is not simply 'what is happening?' but 'how do we organize our knowledge and values in relation to this shift?' — and ultimately, how do we become good ancestors by orienting ourselves collectively toward wisdom and well-being in the back half of the 21st century.

## Apprentice

Throughout the history of life on Earth, there have been a handful of moments where something fundamentally new emerged — not just a better version of what came before, but an entirely new layer of complexity. Cells learned to communicate and multicellular life appeared. Nervous systems arose and animals developed rich behavior. Humans developed language and culture was born. Each time, a new kind of network for processing and sharing information created a whole new dimension of existence.

The argument here is that we are living through the next such moment right now. Artificial intelligence, the internet, and global digital systems are weaving together into a network that may be generating a new plane of complexity at the scale of civilization itself. This isn't just about better technology — it's a structural shift in how information flows and organizes across the entire planet.

What makes this both thrilling and alarming is that we're inside the transition without a clear map. The critical question, as posed by the UTOK framework developed by Gregg Henriques, is not just what is happening but how we organize our understanding and values to meet this moment — and whether we can orient ourselves toward wisdom and well-being so that future generations inherit something worth having.

## Adept

The Tree of Knowledge framework, developed by Gregg Henriques as a core element of UTOK (Unified Theory of Knowledge), identifies a recurring structural pattern across evolutionary history: the emergence of novel information-processing and communication networks generates qualitatively new complex adaptive planes of existence. Matter gave rise to Life through cellular communication. Neuronal networks gave rise to Mind and behavioral complexity. Propositional language gave rise to Culture — the person-culture plane. Each transition represents a genuine joint point where the architecture of complexity reorganizes at a higher order of integration.

The claim now being advanced is that artificial intelligence, large language models, the internet, and global digital infrastructure constitute a fourth such network — one that may be generating a fifth joint point and a new complex adaptive plane at civilizational scale. This is not reducible to technological acceleration or digital transformation in the conventional sense. It represents a phase transition in the informational architecture of human civilization, analogous in structural significance to the emergence of language itself.

What distinguishes this moment is that we are embedded within the transition without adequate metatheoretical frameworks or shared value structures to navigate it. UTOK frames the central challenge not as prediction but as orientation: how do we achieve sufficient conceptual clarity about the nature of this shift to organize knowledge and values coherently? The aspiration is to cultivate collective wisdom — what Henriques frames as becoming "good ancestors" — by developing the epistemic and ethical infrastructure needed to steward this transition toward well-being rather than fragmentation in the coming decades.

## Magus

Gregg Henriques's Tree of Knowledge (ToK) System posits that the history of behavioral complexity is punctuated by joint points — phase transitions where novel information-processing networks generate emergent complex adaptive planes irreducible to their substrates. The canonical sequence moves from Energy to Matter to Life (via cellular signaling networks), to Mind (via nervous systems enabling sensorimotor loops), to Culture (via propositional language enabling justification systems and the culture-person plane). Each joint point instantiates a new ontological dimension governed by its own selection dynamics and organizational logic. The ToK thus functions not merely as taxonomy but as a metatheoretical architecture for understanding the nested, emergent structure of existence.

The provocation now is that digital computation, artificial intelligence, large language models, and planetary-scale information networks constitute the material substrate for a fifth joint point — a transition generating a new complex adaptive plane at the level of global civilization. This is structurally homologous to prior transitions: just as propositional language enabled the externalization and accumulation of justification systems beyond individual minds, digital-AI networks enable the externalization and recursive processing of knowledge at scales and speeds that exceed human cognitive architecture. The claim is ontological, not merely sociological: what is emerging is not better tools but a new kind of informational entity with its own emergent dynamics.

The epistemic crisis embedded in this transition is that humanity lacks both the metatheoretical coherence and the axiological orientation to navigate it. UTOK frames this as the central problem of the 21st century: achieving a unified understanding of knowledge, value, and complexity sufficient to steward a civilizational phase transition. The normative horizon — becoming "good ancestors" — demands not just descriptive adequacy but prescriptive wisdom, integrating epistemology and ethics at the scale the moment requires.
