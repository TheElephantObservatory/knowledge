# The Sacred as Inexhaustible Disclosure, Not a Destination to Reach

The horizon moves because it must.

---

> The sacred is not a destination we arrive at but the inexhaustible process by which reality keeps disclosing itself — always opening new depths as old confusions resolve. Faith becomes trust in this process, and imagination becomes the faculty adequate to what perpetually exceeds our grasp.

The sacred can be reconceived not as a perfect, complete, or fully attainable object — not as an Omega point we will one day reach — but as the inexhaustible process of reality disclosing itself through an iterative, recursive dynamic of illumination and recession. If we take seriously both the incompletability of relevance realization and the radical uncertainty built into reality itself, then the sacred is better understood as the very process by which more of reality becomes intelligible — a process that simultaneously opens new depths as it resolves old confusions. The horizon always recedes as you approach it. This is not a deficiency of the process; it is its essential character. The appropriate orientation toward the sacred is therefore not one of eventual arrival but of inexhaustible participation. The apophatic mystical traditions — which insist that whatever you say about the divine, the divine exceeds it — are not merely expressing epistemic humility; they are tracking something structurally real about the nature of intelligibility itself. This reframing also transforms what it means to have faith: faith is no longer belief in propositions not yet seen, but a deepening trust in the profound logic of the disclosing process itself. As things become seen, they engender a deeper faith — not a termination of the search but an intensification of the orientation toward what remains to be seen. The sacred mystery is not a gap waiting to be filled by future knowledge; it is the inexhaustible fount from which intelligibility perpetually springs. Crucially, this is also why the imaginal is an epistemically indispensable faculty — the capacity to use image, symbol, and myth in a way that sensitizes us to what exceeds our current assimilatory schemas and draws us toward accommodation with a reality that always recedes from full grasp. The imaginal is precisely that mode of cognition which understands that whatever it is grasping is simultaneously withdrawing. This means that liberating the imagination from its contemporary reduction to mere mental imagery is not a concession to irrationality; it is a precondition for the kind of rationality adequate to a multi-layered reality. The sacred cannot be adequately expressed in purely propositional form, not because it is irrational, but because it is richer than any single register of expression can contain. The task of a reconstructive religious project is therefore to find symbolic forms that can carry meaning across all ontological and epistemic levels simultaneously — forms crisp enough at the logos level to make reliable truth claims, and resonant enough at the mythos level to be embodied, lived, and gotten out of bed for.

## Apprentice

What if the sacred isn't something we'll finally reach one day — a finish line, a final answer, a moment of total clarity? What if it's actually the ongoing process by which things become meaningful to us, a process that never ends because every new understanding opens up new mysteries? The horizon moves as you walk toward it. That's not a flaw — it's the whole point.

This reframes what faith means. Instead of believing in things you can't see yet, faith becomes a growing trust in the process of discovery itself. The more you understand, the more you sense there is to understand, and that deepening awareness is what draws you forward. The great mystical traditions that say "whatever you say about God, God is more than that" weren't just being modest — they were describing how understanding actually works.

This also explains why imagination matters so much. Not imagination as idle daydreaming, but as the ability to use stories, symbols, and images to reach toward what we can't yet put into plain words. The sacred is too rich for any single way of speaking to capture it. A living spiritual practice needs forms that are both intellectually honest and emotionally powerful enough to actually shape how people live.

## Adept

This perspective reframes the sacred not as an Omega point — a final state of complete knowledge or union — but as the inexhaustible process of reality disclosing itself through iterative cycles of illumination and recession. If relevance realization is structurally incompletable, and if reality itself harbors radical uncertainty at every scale, then the sacred names the very dynamic by which intelligibility emerges while simultaneously generating new depths that exceed current grasp. The apophatic traditions are not merely exercising epistemic humility; they are tracking a structural feature of intelligibility itself — that whatever is grasped is simultaneously withdrawing.

This reframing transforms faith from propositional belief in unseen realities into a deepening trust in the profound logic of the disclosing process. As understanding grows, it does not terminate the search but intensifies orientation toward what remains unseen. Sacred mystery is not a gap awaiting future knowledge but the inexhaustible source from which intelligibility perpetually springs. The relationship between the known and the unknown is generative, not adversarial.

Crucially, this account renders the imaginal epistemically indispensable. The imaginal — the capacity to deploy image, symbol, and myth in ways that sensitize cognition to what exceeds current assimilatory schemas — is precisely the mode of knowing adequate to a reality that always recedes from full conceptual capture. Liberating imagination from its modern reduction to mere mental imagery is not a concession to irrationality but a precondition for rationality adequate to a multi-layered reality. The task of any reconstructive sacred project becomes finding symbolic forms that operate across ontological registers simultaneously — rigorous enough at the logos level for reliable truth claims, resonant enough at the mythos level to be embodied and lived.

## Magus

The argument here performs a decisive inversion of classical sacred teleology: the sacred is relocated from the terminal position — the Omega point, the beatific vision, the eschatological plenitude — to the generative process itself, understood as the recursive, self-transcending dynamic by which reality discloses intelligibility while simultaneously engendering new domains of opacity. If relevance realization is structurally incompletable (per Vervaeke's framework) and if ontological uncertainty is not merely epistemic limitation but a feature of reality's own constitution, then sacredness names the inexhaustible fount of intelligibility rather than its final product. The apophatic traditions — Pseudo-Dionysius, Eckhart, the Cloud author — are here read not as expressions of pious agnosticism but as precise phenomenological reports on the withdrawal structure intrinsic to intelligibility as such.

This reconception transforms faith from doxastic commitment to unseen propositions into participatory trust in the logos of disclosure — a trust that deepens precisely as understanding deepens, because each resolution of confusion simultaneously opens new depths requiring accommodation. The known-unknown relation is rendered generative rather than privative: mystery is not absence of knowledge but the condition of its perpetual emergence.

The imaginal faculty becomes epistemically non-negotiable within this framework. If reality structurally exceeds any given assimilatory schema, then the mode of cognition that operates through symbol, image, and myth — grasping what it simultaneously recognizes as withdrawing — is not supplementary to propositional rationality but constitutive of any rationality adequate to a multi-leveled, participatory ontology. The contemporary reduction of imagination to subjective mental imagery is thus diagnosed as a catastrophic narrowing of cognitive capacity. The constructive challenge becomes the creation of symbolic forms that achieve simultaneous adequacy across registers: propositionally crisp enough at the logos level for reliable truth-tracking, and mythically resonant enough to function as lived, embodied, motivationally binding orientations toward the inexhaustible.
