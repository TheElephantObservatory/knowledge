# How Propositional Language Made Humans Justifying Primates

The question that changed everything: how do you know?

---

> Once language allows propositional claims, listeners can demand justification — and that demand reshapes everything. The cycle of claim, challenge, and justification becomes a new evolutionary engine, driving cultural accumulation at the level of ideas rather than genes.

Justification is the hinge on which human cultural evolution turns. When animal communication crosses the threshold into grammatical, propositional language — when you can say not just 'gazelle' but 'there is a gazelle over the hill' — something new becomes possible: your interlocutor can say 'how do you know?' That negative space, the space opened by the possibility of questioning a claim, is what Greg Henriques calls justification systems theory. It creates an entirely new evolutionary pressure. You cannot simply assert; you must back it up. That dynamic — claim, challenge, justification — is the engine of human intellectual and cultural evolution. It drives collaboration, competition, and the ratcheting accumulation of knowledge, because each generation of justifications becomes the substrate for the next. Whatever language came from, we know what it does: it makes us justifying primates. And that capacity, once online, generates a new layer of Darwinian evolution — not genetic, but memetic — operating at the level of ideas rather than organisms.

## Apprentice

Animal communication can signal danger or food, but human language does something radically different: it lets us make claims about things that aren't right in front of us. You can say "there's a gazelle over the hill," and crucially, someone can respond, "how do you know?" That simple question changes everything.

The moment you can be challenged to back up what you say, a new pressure kicks in. You can't just assert — you have to justify. Greg Henriques calls this dynamic "justification systems theory." The cycle of making a claim, having it questioned, and offering reasons in response becomes the engine that drives human culture forward. Each round of justification builds on the last, creating a ratchet effect where knowledge accumulates across generations.

This means that whatever originally gave rise to language, its deepest consequence is making us creatures who justify. That capacity opens up an entirely new kind of evolution — not of bodies or genes, but of ideas. Concepts compete, get challenged, survive or fail based on how well they hold up to scrutiny. It's Darwinian logic applied to culture itself.

## Adept

Greg Henriques's justification systems theory identifies a pivotal threshold in the emergence of human cognition: the transition from indexical animal signaling to grammatical, propositional language. When communication becomes capable of asserting states of affairs — "there is a gazelle over the hill" rather than merely "gazelle!" — it simultaneously opens a negative space: the possibility of challenge. The interlocutor can now ask, "how do you know?" That question is not incidental to language; it is the hinge on which human cultural evolution turns.

The claim-challenge-justification cycle constitutes a novel selection environment. Assertions that cannot withstand scrutiny are discarded; those that survive become the substrate for subsequent claims. This ratcheting dynamic explains the cumulative character of human knowledge in a way that neither genetic evolution nor simple social learning can. Each generation inherits not just information but the justificatory structures that validate it, creating layered architectures of reasoning — from folk epistemology to formal science.

The implication is that language, whatever its phylogenetic origins, inaugurates a genuinely new evolutionary domain. Henriques positions justification as the mechanism through which memetic or cultural evolution acquires its Darwinian character: ideas vary, face selective pressure through interpersonal and institutional challenge, and are differentially retained. Humans are, in this framing, fundamentally justifying primates — organisms whose cognitive niche is defined not by what they know but by the demand that they account for how they know it.

## Magus

Henriques's justification systems theory locates the decisive rupture in hominin cognitive evolution not at the emergence of language per se, but at the moment propositional structure enables the pragmatic act of epistemic challenge. The shift from holistic or indexical signaling to compositional semantics — from 'gazelle' to 'there is a gazelle over the hill' — is simultaneously the shift from communication to accountability. The negative space opened by the question "how do you know?" constitutes a new fitness landscape operating on representational content rather than phenotypic traits. This is not metaphor: the claim-challenge-justification triad instantiates variation, selection, and retention at the cultural level with genuine Darwinian fidelity.

What makes this framework particularly incisive is its identification of justification as both product and medium of cumulative cultural evolution. Each justificatory structure — whether a kinship norm, a theological doctrine, or a scientific paradigm — serves as the substrate upon which subsequent claims are erected and against which they are tested. The ratchet operates not merely through information transmission but through the inheritance of evaluative standards. Tomasello's cultural ratchet gains a mechanism; Boyd and Richerson's dual inheritance acquires an epistemic dimension that neither genetic nor purely imitative transmission can furnish.

The deeper philosophical yield is an ontological claim about human nature: to be a language-using primate is to be a justifying primate, and justification is not an epiphenomenon of sociality but its generative engine. Once the justificatory loop is online, it produces emergent selection pressures — institutional, rhetorical, scientific — that recursively reshape the cognitive ecology in which further justification occurs. Culture, on this account, is not merely accumulated knowledge but accumulated accountability, and its evolution is irreducibly normative.
