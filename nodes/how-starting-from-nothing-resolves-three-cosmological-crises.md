# How Starting From Nothing Resolves Three Cosmological Crises

The dart God never had to throw

---

> What if the universe didn't begin as an infinitely dense point but emerged from literally nothing — the quantum vacuum — solving the mystery of low initial entropy, the singularity problem, and matter-antimatter asymmetry in a single stroke?

The standard cosmological model faces a profound and rarely acknowledged problem called the past hypothesis: the second law of thermodynamics tells us that entropy always increases, which means the early universe must have been in an extraordinarily low-entropy state — so improbable that Roger Penrose asked how God could have thrown such an accurate dart. The singularity problem compounds this: if the universe began as an infinitely dense point, why didn't it immediately collapse into a black hole? A different starting point offers simultaneous resolution: begin with no spacetime and no matter — only the quantum vacuum and the laws of particle physics as potentia, as Heisenberg's ontologically real possibilities. From nothing, virtual quark-antiquark pairs spontaneously emerge and return to the vacuum. If two such pairs interact before annihilating, they delay their return — and this delay, under a proposed working hypothesis, triggers a second-order phase transition in which matter steals energy from the vacuum and spacetime begins to be constructed by the matter itself. Starting from nothing, the entropy of the initial state is zero — the past hypothesis is automatically answered. There is no singularity because there is no initial mass concentrated in a point — matter and spacetime co-emerge particle by particle. And matter-antimatter symmetry is broken stochastically, by founder effect, solving baryogenesis. Three of the deepest unsolved problems in cosmology dissolve simultaneously from a single starting assumption.

## Apprentice

The Big Bang story has a hidden problem most people never hear about. If the universe started as everything crushed into one tiny point, why didn't it just become a black hole? And why was the early universe so extraordinarily orderly — so improbable that physicist Roger Penrose compared it to God throwing a dart and hitting a bullseye the size of an atom?

A different starting point dissolves these puzzles at once. Instead of beginning with all matter packed into a point, begin with nothing at all — no space, no time, no matter. Only the quantum vacuum and the laws of physics exist as pure possibilities. In this vacuum, pairs of particles and antiparticles constantly flicker in and out of existence. Normally they vanish instantly. But if two such pairs happen to interact before disappearing, they linger — and that lingering triggers a cascade in which matter begins pulling energy from the vacuum and building spacetime around itself, particle by particle.

Starting from nothing means the initial disorder is zero — the orderliness puzzle is answered automatically. There is no singularity because matter was never crushed into a point; it emerged gradually. And the slight excess of matter over antimatter that makes our universe possible happened by chance — like the first settlers of an island determining its future character. Three deep mysteries, one elegant starting assumption.

## Adept

The standard cosmological model harbors three intertwined problems that are rarely confronted together. The past hypothesis demands an explanation for the extraordinarily low-entropy initial state required by the second law of thermodynamics — a state so improbable that Penrose calculated its likelihood at roughly one part in 10^{10^{123}}. The singularity problem asks why an initial state of infinite density and curvature did not simply remain a black hole. And baryogenesis — the observed matter-antimatter asymmetry — still lacks a consensus mechanism despite decades of effort.

The proposal examined here replaces the initial singularity with a radically minimal ontology: no spacetime, no matter — only the quantum vacuum and the laws of particle physics existing as Heisenberg's potentia, ontologically real possibilities awaiting actualization. Virtual quark-antiquark pairs spontaneously emerge and annihilate. When two such pairs interact before annihilating, their return to the vacuum is delayed. This delay, under the working hypothesis, initiates a second-order phase transition in which matter extracts energy from the vacuum and spacetime is constructed by the matter itself — a co-emergence rather than an expansion from a pre-existing point.

The explanatory payoff is striking. An initial state of literal nothingness carries zero entropy, dissolving the past hypothesis without additional postulates. No singularity arises because there is no concentrated mass at any moment — matter and geometry bootstrap each other incrementally. And matter-antimatter symmetry is broken stochastically through a founder effect: whichever species gains a slight numerical advantage in the earliest interactions dominates the subsequent cascade. Three foundational problems resolve simultaneously from a single ontological starting assumption.

## Magus

Penrose's dart — the staggering fine-tuning implicit in the Weyl curvature hypothesis and the thermodynamic past hypothesis — remains one of cosmology's deepest embarrassments, quietly sidestepped rather than solved. Compounding it, the initial singularity of classical FLRW cosmology is not merely a coordinate artifact but an ontological lacuna: infinite density at t = 0 offers no dynamical account of why gravitational collapse into a black hole was avoided. Meanwhile, baryogenesis continues to resist resolution through Sakharov's conditions alone, with CP violation in the Standard Model insufficient by orders of magnitude.

The framework under consideration proposes a radical deflationary ontology for the initial state: no manifold, no metric, no matter fields — only the quantum vacuum understood as a space of potentia in Heisenberg's sense, where the laws of particle physics specify the symmetry structure of possible actualization. Virtual quark-antiquark pairs undergo spontaneous pair creation and annihilation. The critical hypothesis is that interaction between two such pairs prior to annihilation introduces a delay sufficient to trigger a second-order phase transition — matter condensing energy from the vacuum while simultaneously constructing the spacetime manifold it inhabits. This is co-emergence in a strong sense: geometry is not a background but an emergent product of matter's self-organization.

The triple resolution follows with notable parsimony. The Boltzmann entropy of the initial state is identically zero — not fine-tuned but tautological, since there are no microstates to count. The singularity is eliminated not by quantum gravity corrections to the Friedmann equations but by removing the premise: mass-energy is never concentrated at a point. Baryon asymmetry arises via stochastic symmetry breaking — a founder effect in which the first persistent quark flavor statistically seeds subsequent pair production, analogous to spontaneous magnetization near a critical point. The proposal trades one assumption — nothingness as initial condition — for the dissolution of three problems that have resisted independent solution for decades.
