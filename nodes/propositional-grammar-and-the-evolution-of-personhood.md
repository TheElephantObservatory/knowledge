# Propositional Grammar and the Evolution of Personhood

When the nervous system escaped the skin

---

> Human language didn't just improve on animal communication — it created an entirely new kind of mind. The moment we could make claims others could challenge, justification became the engine that turned conscious animals into self-aware persons.

Symbolic language represents a genuinely unprecedented rupture in the history of meaning-making — not merely an upgrade to animal communication, but the emergence of an entirely new plane of complexity. What makes it so radical is a single structural feature: propositional grammar. The shift from 'gazelle there' to 'a gazelle is there' seems trivial on the surface, but it is cosmically significant. The moment you make a propositional claim, you open a discursive space of challenge and counter-challenge — 'Is it? Are you sure? How do you know?' This is the birth of justification as an adaptive pressure. Before language, even the most socially sophisticated animals remained sealed within their own individual meaning-making frames. But propositional language tears down the wall isolating meaning within the individual mind, allowing the originary motivating content of one mind to become a shared object of concern for another. Linguistic propositions have a unique property: they can pass through the nervous system, cross the boundary of the skin, enter the world, and return — without losing their informational form. Genetic learning operates at the level of the species; cognitive learning at the level of the individual organism. But symbolic learning allows ontogenetic knowledge — built up within a single lifetime — to move fluidly between individuals and collectives. Justification is therefore not merely a social nicety. It is the core mechanism by which meaning becomes collectively linguistic, and by which human beings crossed the threshold from minded animals into genuinely enculturated persons. This transformation also reshapes the inner architecture of the individual: once human beings could be asked 'Why did you do that?', a new kind of self had to come into existence to answer. You cannot justify your behavior to another without first developing a reflective stance toward your own internal processes — a higher-order perspective on your own intentions and desires. This is the origin of self-consciousness, structurally distinct from mere animal consciousness. Out of the unreflective experiential self, the ego is born: the self-conscious inner narrator who models experience in language. A tripartite model of the human psyche follows — the pre-linguistic experiential self, the private ego, and the public persona constructed within the intersubjective justificatory arena of culture. Human beings are therefore not simply conscious — they are persons, constituted by their ongoing participation in systems of justification.

## Apprentice

Animals communicate, but they cannot argue. A vervet monkey's alarm call carries meaning, but no other monkey can respond with 'Are you sure? How do you know?' The moment human language gained the ability to make claims — proper sentences with a subject and a predicate — it opened a door that had never existed before: the door of challenge and justification. That tiny grammatical shift, from a signal to a proposition, changed everything.

Because propositions can be questioned, they force the speaker to develop reasons. And because they can travel from one mind to another without losing their shape, knowledge stopped being trapped inside individual heads. For the first time, what one person learned in their lifetime could be examined, corrected, and built upon by others. This is something neither genes nor individual brains could do alone.

This process also rebuilt the inner life of each person. Once you can be asked 'Why did you do that?', you need a self capable of answering — a reflective inner narrator who can look at your own desires and intentions from the outside. That narrator is what we call the ego, and it is born from the pressure of justification. Human beings are not just conscious creatures; they are persons, shaped by their lifelong participation in the shared practice of giving and asking for reasons.

## Adept

The emergence of propositional grammar marks a genuine discontinuity in the history of mind — not a quantitative improvement on prior signaling systems, but a phase transition into a new order of complexity. The structural key is the proposition itself: a syntactically bound claim that carries truth conditions and therefore invites epistemic challenge. Where indexical signals like alarm calls are closed, self-validating, and bound to immediate context, propositional utterances open a discursive space of justification — 'Is it? How do you know?' — that has no analogue in non-human communication.

This discursive openness has a profound consequence for the transmission of knowledge. Genetic learning is phylogenetic; individual learning is ontogenetic and dies with the organism. Propositional language introduces a third register: ontogenetically acquired knowledge that can cross the boundary of the individual nervous system, enter a shared intersubjective space, and be preserved, corrected, and accumulated across generations. Justification is the mechanism that makes this transmission reliable — it is the filter through which claims are tested before being incorporated into collective understanding.

The implications for selfhood are equally radical. The pressure to justify one's behavior to others necessitates a reflective, higher-order stance toward one's own mental states — the capacity to treat one's own intentions and desires as objects of scrutiny. This is the structural origin of self-consciousness, distinct from mere phenomenal awareness. A tripartite architecture of the psyche follows: the pre-linguistic experiential self, the private ego constituted through inner narrative, and the public persona negotiated within the intersubjective arena of culture. Personhood, on this account, is not a biological given but an achievement of participation in normative practices of reason-giving.

## Magus

The argument advanced here locates the anthropological rupture not in tool use, sociality, or even symbolic representation per se, but in the advent of propositional syntax — the grammatical structure that binds referring expressions to predicate terms under conditions of assertoric force. The proposition is the minimal unit that can be true or false, and it is precisely this truth-aptness that generates what might be called the justificatory dialectic: the inferential space of challenge, counter-claim, and warrant-demand that Brandom and Sellars identify as the normative bedrock of discursive practice. Pre-propositional signals, however semantically rich, remain causally embedded in their contexts; they cannot be detached, negated, embedded in conditionals, or subjected to the question of grounds.

The epistemological consequence is a transformation in the architecture of learning itself. The tripartite schema — phylogenetic, ontogenetic, and what might be termed 'logogenetic' or culturally transmitted knowledge — identifies propositional language as the medium through which ontogenetically acquired representational content achieves intersubjective stability. The proposition is the vehicle that allows informational structure to traverse the boundary of the organism, persist in the shared semiotic environment, and re-enter individual cognition without degradation of form. Justification functions here not as a post-hoc social gloss on private cognition but as the constitutive mechanism of collective epistemic rationality.

The consequences for the theory of subjectivity are equally far-reaching. The demand for justification — 'Why did you do that?' — imposes a recursive, second-order structure on the agent's relation to her own conative and doxastic states. This reflexive self-modeling is the structural condition for what Hegel calls Geist and what phenomenological traditions distinguish as self-consciousness proper, irreducible to mere sentience. The resulting tripartite psyche — pre-reflective experiential subject, private ego constituted through inner propositional narrative, and socially negotiated persona — maps onto the Freudian topography while grounding it in a philosophy of language rather than a hydraulic energetics. Personhood emerges as a normative status, conferred and sustained through ongoing participation in the space of reasons.
