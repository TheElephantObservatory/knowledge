# Shared Cosmic Narratives and Civilizational Value

Recovering the memory of the future

---

> Civilization's deepest crisis isn't economic or political — it's the collapse of a shared story that gives life meaning. Cosmoerotic Humanism calls this a Global Intimacy Disorder, and argues the only way forward is rebuilding a cosmic narrative no single institution can hijack.

The deepest root of the meta-crisis is what Cosmoerotic Humanism calls a Global Intimacy Disorder — a fundamental breakdown in a shared story of value. To understand this, we must distinguish three levels of civilizational analysis: infrastructure, social structure, and superstructure. Most crisis-thinkers focus on the first two — material systems, laws, governance. But Cosmoerotic Humanism locates the core crisis in superstructure: the collapse of what Charles Taylor calls the 'inescapable framework' inside which human beings orient themselves, make meaning, and act. That framework has a narrative structure — a story with three temporal dimensions: a shared memory of the past, a way of engaging the present, and what one Hasidic master called a 'memory of the future.' When a couple loses their shared story, they lose intimacy. The same is happening at civilizational scale. When the shared story of value collapses, you get a Global Intimacy Disorder — and nature abhors a vacuum. Something else rushes in to fill it. But before we can responsibly reconstruct a shared cosmic story, we must understand why modernity rejected the very idea of one. In pre-modernity, the narrative structure of reality was weaponized: particular religious institutions hijacked the cosmic plotline, each claiming to be its central character, using that claim to reify power and justify domination. The wars, persecutions, and coercions that followed were not incidental — they were structurally produced by the logic of exclusive cosmic authorship. Modernity, led by figures like Voltaire, made a reasonable bet: strip out the plotline, reject telos, disenchant the cosmos, so no institution could ever again claim to be its authorized interpreter. This was a genuine correction — but it carried a catastrophic cost, leaving human beings inside a cosmos with no direction, no story, no shared memory of the future. The task now is neither to return to pre-modern plotlines nor to remain in the modern rejection of telos, but to reconstruct a post-critical, post-rivalrous account of cosmic direction — one grounded in evolving first principles accessible to all and impossible for any single institution to hijack. This is precisely what Cosmoerotic Humanism is attempting to provide.

## Apprentice

Most people trying to understand what's going wrong in the world focus on broken systems — failing governments, collapsing economies, environmental destruction. But there's a deeper problem underneath all of these: human beings have lost a shared story about what matters and why. Cosmoerotic Humanism calls this a Global Intimacy Disorder. Just as a couple who loses their shared story loses their intimacy, a civilization that loses its shared story of value begins to fall apart from the inside.

This didn't happen by accident. Before the modern era, religions and institutions claimed to own the story of reality — and they used that ownership to justify wars, persecution, and control. Thinkers like Voltaire made a reasonable decision: strip out the grand story entirely, so no one could ever weaponize it again. That move protected people from a lot of harm, but it left everyone inside a universe that points nowhere and means nothing.

The challenge now is to find a third path — not returning to the old stories that were so easily captured by powerful institutions, and not staying stuck in a world with no story at all. The goal is to reconstruct a shared sense of cosmic direction built on principles that anyone can examine and that no single group can claim to own exclusively.

## Adept

Cosmoerotic Humanism situates the meta-crisis not primarily at the level of infrastructure or social structure — material systems, institutional arrangements, governance failures — but at the level of superstructure: the collapse of what Charles Taylor calls the 'inescapable framework,' the orienting horizon within which human beings make meaning, assign value, and act with purpose. This framework is not merely philosophical; it has a narrative structure with three temporal dimensions — a shared memory of the past, a mode of engaging the present, and what one Hasidic tradition names a 'memory of the future.' When that narrative collapses at civilizational scale, the result is a Global Intimacy Disorder: the loss of the shared story of value that makes collective life coherent.

The modern rejection of this narrative framework was not arbitrary. Pre-modern cosmologies were routinely weaponized: particular institutions claimed exclusive authorship of the cosmic plotline and used that claim to consolidate power, justify persecution, and suppress dissent. Modernity's disenchantment project — its deliberate stripping of telos from the cosmos — was a structural correction to this abuse. But the correction carried a catastrophic cost: it left human beings inhabiting a universe without direction, and nature abhors a vacuum. Rival pseudo-narratives — nationalist, ideological, algorithmic — rush in to fill the void, often reproducing the very domination dynamics the Enlightenment sought to escape.

The constructive proposal is to move beyond both pre-modern capture and modern rejection toward a post-critical, post-rivalrous account of cosmic direction — one grounded in evolving first principles that are publicly accessible and structurally resistant to institutional hijacking. This is the explicit ambition of Cosmoerotic Humanism's reconstructive project.

## Magus

The diagnostic framework offered here operates at the level of what might be called civilizational depth ontology. Distinguishing infrastructure, social structure, and superstructure as analytically separable registers of civilizational analysis, Cosmoerotic Humanism locates the generative root of the meta-crisis in the superstructural collapse of what Charles Taylor theorizes as the 'inescapable framework' — the pre-theoretical horizon of strong evaluation within which moral agency, meaning-making, and temporal self-orientation become possible. This framework is not merely axiological but narratological: it possesses three temporal valences — anamnetic, presentic, and what the Hasidic tradition figures as a 'memory of the future,' a proleptic orientation toward telos. The civilizational loss of this tripartite narrative structure constitutes what the framework names a Global Intimacy Disorder — a structural homology between the relational breakdown that occurs when a dyadic shared story dissolves and the civilizational breakdown that occurs when the story of value itself becomes contested or absent.

The genealogy of this collapse is not innocent. Pre-modern cosmological narratives were systematically captured by particular institutional actors who claimed exclusive hermeneutic authority over the cosmic plotline — a structural move that transformed shared orientation into a mechanism of domination, persecution, and inter-communal violence. The Voltairean wager — the deliberate excision of telos, the disenchantment of cosmic directionality — was a rational prophylactic response to this capture dynamic. Yet the cure introduced its own pathology: a cosmos stripped of narrative structure cannot sustain the inescapable frameworks that human agency requires, and the resulting vacuum is colonized by surrogate narratives — nationalist, ideological, algorithmic — that frequently recapitulate the exclusivist and dominating logic they nominally replace.

The reconstructive ambition is accordingly post-critical in the Ricoeurian sense and post-rivalrous in its political logic: to articulate a shared account of cosmic direction grounded in evolving, publicly falsifiable first principles whose structural openness forecloses the possibility of exclusive institutional authorship. This is the precise theoretical and practical stake of the Cosmoerotic Humanism project.
