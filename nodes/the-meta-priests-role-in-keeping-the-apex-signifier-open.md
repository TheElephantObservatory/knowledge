# The Meta-Priest's Role in Keeping the Apex Signifier Open

God as the body knowing itself whole

---

> Knowing God is not primarily an intellectual exercise but a whole-organism event — physical, emotional, and mental at once. The task is to keep that ultimate sense of meaning open and alive without letting it become a totalizing force that overwrites the wellbeing of others.

There is a crucial distinction between the meta-theological mode — the intellectual discussion of what kind of entity God is and how God relates to reality — and the meta-priestly mode, which asks: are you actually experiencing God? That experience is physical, emotional, and mental simultaneously. When the intellectual, emotional, and somatic dimensions are brought into alignment and opened maximally, something like a transcendent immanence becomes available. To be in the mode of knowing God as an organism is, in some fundamental sense, to be happy. The warmth and love that emerges when people confess their love of God is the organism accessing its maximal surplus coherence, which is itself a form of the Divine. The meta-priest's task — operating cross-paradigmatically across Mythic, modern, postmodern, and metamodern world-spaces — is to help keep each person's apex signifier open rather than closed, cultivating conditions under which individuals and communities can access maximal coherent meaning in ways that are healthy, generative, and adequate to the scale of the civilizational challenges we face. The danger is when that apex becomes totalizing — what Lacan called the big other — an obscene oppressive potential whereby someone enacts things in the name of a totality that overwrites everyone else's wellbeing.

## Apprentice

There is a big difference between talking about God as an idea and actually experiencing something divine. The first is an intellectual exercise — debating what God is, how God relates to the world. The second is something felt in the body, the emotions, and the mind all at once. When all three of those dimensions line up and open fully, people often describe a feeling of deep warmth, love, and coherence — a sense that everything fits together. That feeling, this perspective argues, is itself a form of contact with the divine.

The role of what might be called a "meta-priest" is to help people access that experience regardless of their background — whether they come from a traditional religious framework, a secular one, or something in between. The goal is to keep each person's deepest source of meaning alive and open rather than rigid and closed.

But there is a real danger here. When someone's ultimate sense of meaning becomes absolute and totalizing — when it turns into a force that justifies overriding other people's freedom and wellbeing — it becomes oppressive. The art lies in cultivating the most profound coherence a person can access while ensuring it remains generous, healthy, and responsive to the enormous challenges facing the world today.

## Adept

A critical distinction separates the meta-theological mode — intellectual inquiry into the nature of God and God's relationship to reality — from the meta-priestly mode, which concerns whether a person is actually experiencing the divine. This experience is not purely cognitive; it is simultaneously somatic, emotional, and intellectual. When these three dimensions achieve alignment and maximal openness, something described as transcendent immanence becomes available. The warmth and love people express when confessing their love of God reflects the organism accessing its maximal surplus coherence — a state that is, in this framing, itself a form of the Divine. To know God as an organism, in this fundamental sense, is to be happy.

The meta-priest operates cross-paradigmatically — across Mythic, modern, postmodern, and metamodern world-spaces — with a specific task: keeping each person's apex signifier open rather than closed. The apex signifier is whatever functions as someone's ultimate horizon of meaning, the highest-order concept around which their sense-making organizes. The meta-priestly function cultivates conditions under which individuals and communities can access maximal coherent meaning in ways that are healthy, generative, and adequate to civilizational-scale challenges.

The corresponding danger is well-mapped by Lacanian psychoanalysis: when the apex signifier becomes totalizing — functioning as what Lacan called the big Other — it carries an obscene oppressive potential. Someone acting in the name of an absolute totality can overwrite the wellbeing of everyone around them. The discipline of the meta-priestly mode lies precisely in holding the apex open, ensuring that maximal coherence never collapses into maximal coercion.

## Magus

The distinction between meta-theology and meta-priesthood marks a decisive shift in register: from second-order discourse about the ontological status of the Divine to the phenomenological and organismic question of whether transcendence is being enacted in real time. Meta-theology adjudicates propositions about God's nature and relational topology vis-à-vis reality; the meta-priestly mode asks whether the knowing subject is somatically, affectively, and noetically integrated in a manner that opens onto what can be termed transcendent immanence. The claim is that when these three dimensions achieve maximal coherent alignment, the organism accesses its surplus coherence — and this surplus is not merely analogous to but constitutive of a mode of the Divine. To know God as an organism is, irreducibly, to instantiate happiness as ontological participation.

The meta-priest's operational domain is necessarily cross-paradigmatic, traversing Mythic, modern, postmodern, and metamodern world-spaces without collapsing into any single grammar of ultimacy. The task is the curation of each person's apex signifier — that which functions as the highest-order attractor in their meaning-making architecture — keeping it open, generative, and adequate to the scale of coherence demanded by civilizational crisis. This is not pastoral care in the conventional sense but a structural intervention at the level of semiotic topology.

The pathology is equally precise. When the apex signifier totalizes — when it begins to function as what Lacan theorized as the big Other — it generates an obscene jouissance that authorizes the subject to enact its totality at the expense of all competing coherences. The meta-priestly discipline thus operates on a razor's edge: maximizing organismic coherence while rigorously preventing its closure into a totalizing signifying regime that overwrites alterity and forecloses the wellbeing of others.
