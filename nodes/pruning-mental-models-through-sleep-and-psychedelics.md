# Pruning Mental Models through Sleep and Psychedelics

Shedding the heavy architecture of the self

---

> Sleep, dreaming, and psychedelics are all ways the brain manages the complexity of its own models — pruning what it no longer needs, rehearsing what matters, or temporarily dissolving the compressed shortcuts that normally structure perception.

Sleep, dreaming, and psychedelic states can all be understood through the free energy principle as different modes of complexity minimization. Variational free energy decomposes into accuracy minus complexity. When sensory input is absent — as in sleep — there is no accuracy term, so sleep affords the opportunity to minimize model complexity without the constraint of remaining accurate to incoming data. This is why we sleep: to discard overly parameterized generative models, pruning synaptic associations that embody redundant structure, so we do not overfit our data. Dreaming is the rehearsal of causal structure during this pruning process. Psychedelics work by a complementary mechanism: drugs like psilocybin dissolve the precision afforded to high-level abstract prior beliefs — compressed iconic representations like 'microphone' or 'self' — thereby relatively enhancing the precision of elemental sensory inputs. You no longer perceive the microphone; you perceive the ensemble of surfaces and reflections you would otherwise assemble into one. At the deepest level of abstraction, the story that you are a self can also dissolve — producing non-dual states, depersonalization, or the oceanic feeling, depending on dose and context.

## Apprentice

The brain doesn't just passively record the world — it builds internal models to predict what's coming next. Over time, those models can become bloated, full of redundant patterns that don't really add much. Sleep appears to be the brain's opportunity to tidy up: with no incoming information demanding attention, it can quietly discard the excess without worrying about getting predictions wrong in the moment. Dreaming, on this view, is what that pruning process looks like from the inside — a rehearsal of the causal patterns worth keeping.

Psychedelic drugs like psilocybin work differently but address a related problem. Normally, the brain compresses experience into high-level shortcuts — you glance at an object and instantly perceive 'microphone' rather than the specific play of light and shadow in front of you. Psilocybin appears to loosen the grip of these compressed categories, allowing raw sensory detail to flood back in. At the most abstract level, even the sense of being a unified self is one of these compressed shortcuts — and at sufficient doses, that too can dissolve, producing the oceanic or boundary-dissolving experiences that meditators and psychedelic users have long described.

## Adept

Within the free energy principle framework, the brain is understood as a prediction machine that minimizes variational free energy — a quantity that decomposes into an accuracy term and a complexity term. Accuracy rewards models that fit incoming sensory data; complexity penalizes models with unnecessary parameters. During waking life, both terms are in play. Sleep, however, removes the accuracy constraint entirely: with sensory channels quieted, the brain can minimize complexity alone, pruning synaptic weights that encode redundant or overfitted structure. This is the computational rationale for sleep — not rest, but model compression. Dreaming represents the phenomenology of that compression: the rehearsal and consolidation of causal structure as the generative model is restructured.

Psychedelics operate on the complementary term. Compounds like psilocybin are understood to attenuate the precision weighting assigned to high-level prior beliefs — the compressed, iconic representations that normally dominate perception. When the precision of abstract priors is reduced, the relative precision of low-level sensory prediction errors increases, and perception shifts toward its constituent elements rather than its categorical summaries. The 'microphone' dissolves into surfaces, reflections, and textures. At the apex of the representational hierarchy sits the self-model — the most abstract and compressed prior of all. Dissolving its precision produces the spectrum of non-dual states, depersonalization, and ego dissolution that characterize high-dose psychedelic experience, and that parallel the phenomenology reported in advanced contemplative practice.

## Magus

The free energy principle provides a unifying computational grammar for understanding sleep, dreaming, and psychedelic phenomenology as distinct regimes of inference under a shared objective function. Variational free energy — the quantity the brain is held to minimize — decomposes canonically into a complexity cost and an accuracy term, the latter indexing the fit between posterior beliefs and sensory evidence. During wakefulness, minimization is constrained by continuous afferent input: the accuracy term is non-negligible and the system must balance model fit against parsimony. Sleep instantiates a qualitatively different optimization regime: the attenuation of ascending sensory precision effectively zeros out the accuracy term, permitting unconstrained complexity minimization. The result is Hebbian and synaptic downscaling — the pruning of overdetermined generative model parameters that would otherwise constitute overfitting to the organism's experiential sample. Dreaming, on this account, is the phenomenal correlate of offline model revision: the forward-generative replay of causal structure undergoing consolidation and compression.

Psychedelics perturb the hierarchical precision-weighting architecture rather than the accuracy-complexity balance per se. Serotonergic agonists such as psilocybin are hypothesized to flatten the precision assigned to high-level abstract priors — compressed, context-invariant representations that ordinarily exert strong top-down attenuation on prediction error propagation. The consequent relative upweighting of low-level sensory precision errors produces a phenomenology of perceptual decomposition: categorical objects dissolve into their generative constituents, and the inferential compression that ordinarily produces unified object perception is suspended. The self-model — the most recursively compressed and contextually invariant prior in the hierarchy, encoding the narrative continuity of the subject — is subject to the same mechanism at sufficient doses. Its precision attenuation yields the phenomenological spectrum from depersonalization through ego dissolution to non-dual awareness, states whose structural similarity to advanced samadhi practice suggests convergent routes to the same inferential boundary condition: a posterior in which the subject-prior carries negligible weight.
