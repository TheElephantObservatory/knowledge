# The Neural Process of Constructing Meaningful Worlds

The eye that builds what it sees

---

> How does a nervous system know what matters? Vervaeke's Recursive Relevance Realization explains cognition as a multilevel, self-referential process of simultaneously discovering and constructing meaningful patterns — bridged with evolutionary behavioral theory to produce a precise, unified definition of what cognition actually is.

Vervaeke's concept of Recursive Relevance Realization offers a functional account of how nervous systems — and particularly human nervous systems — are able to know anything at all. Relevance is the process by which an organism picks out what is meaningful in its environment, but crucially there is no general theory of relevance, because relevance is always evolutionary and contextually specific — what matters in one context does not automatically transfer to another. Realization carries a deliberate double meaning: to realize something is both to become aware of it and to construct or create it. This dual sense captures the way cognition is simultaneously receptive and generative, simultaneously discovering affordances and building them. Vervaeke then adds recursivity: this process of relevance realization is happening simultaneously across multiple levels of modeling, from basic perceptual processes up through higher-order representations, all interrelating and feeding back into one another. The result is a functional argument for how neural information processing affords an animal the capacity to navigate its environment — not by processing inputs mechanically, but by continuously and recursively picking out what matters and constructing a world of actionable meaning from it. One of the most generative theoretical moves in UTOK is the bridging of this framework with Behavioral Investment Theory, which situates the animal as a complex adaptive system engaged in deciphering patterns of occurrence in its environment and frames behavior as the process by which an organism expends energy to get a return on that investment — combining evolutionary theory, weak neurocognitive functionalism, and behavioral science. This bridge produces a precise mapping: four levels of responsivity — reactions (reflexive and procedural), learning (perceiving and participating in the environment), thinking (projecting forward across time), and talking (the distinctly human layer of linguistic and propositional cognition) — correspond directly to Vervaeke's recursive levels. The result is a meta-theory that is simultaneously evolutionary, functional, structural, and phenomenological — a genuine synthesis that allows us to finally define what cognition actually means with precision.

## Apprentice

Every living creature faces a version of the same problem: the world contains an overwhelming flood of information, and somehow the animal has to pick out what actually matters to it right now. John Vervaeke calls this process relevance realization. The key insight is that there is no fixed rulebook for what counts as relevant — relevance is always specific to the situation, the creature, and the moment. And crucially, realizing something is not just noticing it passively; it is also partly constructing it, the way a skilled athlete doesn't just see opportunities on a field but actively shapes them.

What makes this framework powerful is the recursive part: this picking-out-what-matters is happening simultaneously at many levels at once, from basic reflexes all the way up to conscious thought, with each level influencing the others in a continuous feedback loop. Cognition, on this view, is not a machine processing inputs — it is a living system continuously building a world of actionable meaning.

This framework becomes even more powerful when connected to Behavioral Investment Theory, which treats an animal as a system spending energy to get returns from its environment. Together, they map four layers of responsivity — reflexes, learning, thinking, and language — onto Vervaeke's recursive levels, producing a unified theory that is at once evolutionary, structural, and experiential.

## Adept

Vervaeke's Recursive Relevance Realization addresses one of the hardest problems in cognitive science: how does a finite nervous system, confronted with an effectively infinite environment, determine what is salient? His answer begins with the observation that relevance has no general theory — it is irreducibly context-sensitive and evolutionarily shaped, meaning no algorithm can precompute what matters. The term 'realization' is deliberately polysemous: it names both the epistemic act of becoming aware and the ontological act of bringing something into being, capturing the way cognition is simultaneously receptive and generative, discovering affordances while also constructing them.

The recursive dimension is where the framework gains its explanatory depth. Relevance realization is not a single-level operation but a nested, self-referential process operating across multiple tiers of neural modeling — from subcortical perceptual binding through higher-order metacognitive representation — with each tier modulating and being modulated by the others. This produces a genuinely dynamic, non-linear account of how meaning emerges from biological information processing.

The theoretical reach of this framework expands considerably when bridged with Behavioral Investment Theory, which models the organism as a complex adaptive system allocating metabolic resources to decode environmental patterns and generate behavioral returns. The synthesis maps four levels of responsivity — reactions, learning, thinking, and talking — onto Vervaeke's recursive hierarchy, integrating evolutionary theory, weak neurocognitive functionalism, and behavioral science into a meta-theoretical architecture that offers a precise, operationalizable definition of cognition itself.

## Magus

Vervaeke's Recursive Relevance Realization constitutes a functional-phenomenological solution to the frame problem as it manifests in biological cognition: given the combinatorial explosion of potentially salient features in any environment, how does a nervous system achieve the bounded, context-sensitive salience landscape that underwrites adaptive behavior? The framework's first move is to deny that relevance admits of a domain-general computational solution — relevance is phylogenetically and ontogenetically indexed, irreducibly tied to the organism's niche and its history of transactions with that niche. 'Realization' is deployed with deliberate semantic density, simultaneously invoking epistemic disclosure and ontological enactment, thereby refusing the classical subject-object dichotomy and positioning cognition within an enactivist-adjacent, affordance-theoretic ontology where perception and construction are co-constitutive.

The recursive architecture is the framework's most technically demanding commitment. Relevance realization is posited as operating concurrently across nested levels of representational and sub-representational processing — from sensorimotor contingency encoding through propositional and metacognitive modeling — with bidirectional constraint propagation across levels. This produces a non-decomposable, dynamically coupled system in which no single level is privileged as the locus of cognition, offering a structural account of how phenomenal salience and functional selectivity co-arise.

The integration with Behavioral Investment Theory effects a crucial naturalistic grounding. BIT's framing of the organism as a metabolically constrained adaptive investor — deciphering environmental regularities to optimize energetic returns — provides the evolutionary and behavioral scaffolding within which Vervaeke's levels find empirical anchoring. The resulting four-tier correspondence (reactions, learning, thinking, talking) maps recursive depth onto phylogenetic and ontogenetic complexity, yielding a meta-theoretical synthesis that is simultaneously evolutionary, neurofunctional, structural, and phenomenological — and that finally renders 'cognition' a term with genuine theoretical precision rather than merely descriptive convenience.
