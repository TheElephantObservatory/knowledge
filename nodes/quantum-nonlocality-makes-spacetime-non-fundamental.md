# Quantum Nonlocality Makes Spacetime Non-Fundamental

The universe has no edges, only entanglements.

---

> The 2022 Nobel Prize confirmed the universe is fundamentally nonlocal. If taken seriously, this single fact eliminates nearly every leading theory of quantum gravity — general relativity, string theory, loop quantum gravity, even holography — because all of them assume spacetime and locality are fundamental.

The 2022 Nobel Prize in Physics confirmed something philosophically staggering that most people have not fully absorbed: the universe is fundamentally nonlocal. The loophole-free Bell test experiments demonstrated that when two entangled particles are separated by any distance, measuring one instantaneously determines the state of the other — not because a signal travels between them, but because they are, in some deep sense, not truly separate. The radical move is to take this seriously as a starting point for cosmology rather than an embarrassing footnote. If nonlocality is fundamental, then locality — the assumption that things can only affect other things through continuous propagation through space — is not fundamental. And if locality is not fundamental, then spacetime itself is not fundamental, because spacetime is precisely the structure that encodes locality. This single move has devastating consequences for virtually every existing approach to quantum gravity. General relativity requires spacetime as fundamental and therefore cannot itself be fundamental. String theory, despite its eleven dimensions, is local on that spacetime and therefore cannot be fundamental. Loop quantum gravity quantizes general relativity and its atoms of spacetime are defined by adjacency — it too is local and therefore cannot be fundamental. Even the holographic principle and AdS/CFT duality are local constructions and fall under the same critique. By starting with nonlocality, you eliminate almost every candidate theory of quantum gravity by your opening assumption — which is either a catastrophic problem or a powerful filter, depending on whether the assumption is correct.

## Apprentice

In 2022, the Nobel Prize in Physics was awarded for experiments proving something extraordinary: two particles, once entangled, instantly share information regardless of the distance between them. No signal passes between them. They simply are not, in any deep sense, truly separate. This property is called nonlocality, and it is not a quirk — it is a confirmed feature of how the universe works.

Most of physics assumes the opposite — that things can only influence each other by sending signals through space, step by step. This assumption is called locality, and it is baked into spacetime itself. Spacetime is essentially the structure that says "things over here can only affect things over there by traveling the distance between them." But if nonlocality is the deeper truth, then spacetime is not the bedrock of reality — it is something that emerges from something else.

This has enormous consequences. Nearly every major attempt to explain gravity at the quantum level — general relativity, string theory, loop quantum gravity, even popular ideas like the holographic principle — treats spacetime as fundamental or builds on structures that assume locality. If you start from nonlocality as your foundation, all of these approaches are ruled out from the very first step. That is either a devastating problem for this line of reasoning or an incredibly powerful way to narrow the search for a true theory of everything.

## Adept

The loophole-free Bell test experiments recognized by the 2022 Nobel Prize in Physics established beyond reasonable doubt that quantum entanglement violates Bell inequalities — confirming that the universe is fundamentally nonlocal. The radical philosophical move is to treat this not as a peculiarity of quantum mechanics but as a foundational constraint on any theory of physics, including quantum gravity. If nonlocality is fundamental, then locality — the principle that causal influence propagates continuously through space — is emergent rather than basic. And since spacetime is precisely the mathematical structure that encodes locality, spacetime itself cannot be fundamental.

This single assumption acts as a devastating filter on existing approaches to quantum gravity. General relativity presupposes a smooth spacetime manifold and is therefore disqualified as a fundamental theory. String theory, despite its extra dimensions, remains a local quantum field theory on a background spacetime. Loop quantum gravity quantizes geometry through spin networks defined by adjacency relations — a fundamentally local construction. Even the holographic principle and AdS/CFT correspondence, which relate bulk gravitational physics to boundary conformal field theories, operate within local frameworks.

The argument's power lies in its economy: a single empirically grounded premise eliminates virtually the entire landscape of quantum gravity candidates. The question becomes whether this is a reductio ad absurdum of taking nonlocality too seriously, or whether it correctly identifies the reason these programs have failed to produce a complete theory. If the latter, the implication is that a successful theory of quantum gravity must be built from intrinsically nonlocal primitives from which spacetime and locality emerge as approximate, large-scale phenomena.

## Magus

The 2022 Nobel recognition of Aspect, Clauser, and Zeilinger's loophole-free Bell inequality violations constitutes an empirical demarcation point whose foundational implications remain radically underprocessed. The experiments do not merely confirm quantum entanglement as an operational resource; they falsify local realism as a feature of nature at the ontological level. The decisive interpretive move is to elevate this result from a constraint on hidden-variable theories to a constraint on fundamental physics itself: if nonlocality is not emergent but primitive, then any structure encoding locality — paradigmatically, the spacetime manifold — is necessarily derivative.

The consequences for quantum gravity research programs are systematically eliminative. General relativity, as a theory of dynamical spacetime geometry, presupposes the very structure that must now be explained. String theory, formulated as a local perturbative expansion on a background manifold (or its nonperturbative M-theoretic extensions), inherits locality at the level of worldsheet or brane dynamics. Loop quantum gravity's spin-network states are defined via adjacency in combinatorial graphs — locality is encoded in their very combinatorics. The AdS/CFT correspondence, often invoked as evidence for emergent spacetime, nonetheless requires a local conformal field theory on the boundary and a local bulk geometry as its dual; the duality reshuffles locality rather than eliminating it.

What emerges is a meta-theoretical criterion of startling parsimony: a single experimentally established premise — fundamental nonlocality — serves as a sieve through which no major extant quantum gravity program passes. The productive question is whether any coherent mathematical framework can be constructed from intrinsically nonlocal primitives such that spacetime, locality, and Lorentz invariance emerge in appropriate limits. This inverts the standard research strategy, which begins with spacetime and attempts to quantize it, replacing it with the demand to derive spacetime from pre-geometric, nonlocal structure.
