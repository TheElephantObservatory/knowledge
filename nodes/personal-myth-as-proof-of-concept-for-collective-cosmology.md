# Personal Myth as Proof of Concept for Collective Cosmology

The alchemist's life is the first experiment

---

> Brendan Graham Dempsey argues that building a new mythology adequate to both science and the human soul requires poets, not scientists — and that the mythmaker's own life inevitably becomes the first test case of the very cosmic story they are trying to tell.

A recurring and underexamined problem in the conversation about meaning, religion, and cultural renewal is what Brendan Graham Dempsey frames as the distinction between personal mythology and collective mythos — and the way in which, for serious thinkers in this space, these two levels become deeply entangled. The core civilizational task, he argues, is to produce an account of the universe that is both in accord with our scientific understanding and psychologically fulfilling — a new mythological rendering of the cosmos that is right both objectively and subjectively. Crucially, this is the task for poets and artists, not scientists, because it must speak to the heart and not just the head, be beautiful and not simply correct. What makes this observation significant is the recognition that the person doing this work inevitably finds their own autobiographical story becoming an instance of the very myth they are trying to generate. The storytelling ego and the cosmic story-generating project collapse into each other. The personal mythology of someone whose life arc moves from scientific materialism through crisis into a new integrative vision is not incidental to the collective mythos they are building — it is its first proof of concept. This is what Dempsey calls metamodern alchemy: updating the alchemical tradition of big-picture symbolic cosmological thinking with the tools and constraints of contemporary knowledge, producing work that is simultaneously deeply idiosyncratic and deeply archetypal, both radically new and connected to very old human patterns of meaning-making.

## Apprentice

One of the deepest challenges of our time is creating a story about the universe that is both scientifically honest and emotionally meaningful — something that speaks to the heart, not just the intellect. Brendan Graham Dempsey argues that this is fundamentally a task for poets and artists rather than scientists, because what's needed isn't just accuracy but beauty and psychological resonance.

What makes this especially interesting is the way the mythmaker's own life gets pulled into the project. Someone who moves from a purely scientific worldview, through a personal crisis, and into a richer vision of meaning doesn't just end up telling a universal story — their own biography becomes the first example of that story working. The personal journey and the cosmic narrative fold into each other.

Dempsey calls this "metamodern alchemy" — an update of the old alchemical tradition of grand symbolic thinking about the cosmos, now informed by modern knowledge. The result is work that feels both deeply personal and deeply archetypal, connecting ancient patterns of meaning-making with genuinely new understanding.

## Adept

Brendan Graham Dempsey identifies a structural entanglement at the heart of contemporary meaning-making: the collapse of personal mythology into collective mythos. The civilizational task, as he frames it, is to generate a cosmological narrative that satisfies both scientific rigor and psychological depth — one that is objectively defensible and subjectively fulfilling. This is not a task for scientists, he insists, but for poets and artists, because the required output must be beautiful, not merely correct. It must move the whole person, not just update their propositional beliefs.

The critical insight is that anyone seriously undertaking this work discovers their own autobiographical arc becoming an instantiation of the myth they are constructing. The storytelling subject and the cosmic narrative project become recursive. A life that moves from scientific materialism through existential crisis into integrative vision is not merely context for the mythological work — it is the myth's first proof of concept. The personal and the universal are not two separate registers but a single self-validating loop.

Dempsey names this process "metamodern alchemy" — a deliberate retrieval of the alchemical tradition's ambition to produce totalizing symbolic cosmologies, now disciplined by contemporary epistemological constraints. The resulting work is simultaneously idiosyncratic and archetypal, radically novel and deeply continuous with ancient patterns of human meaning-making. This framing positions the metamodern mythmaker not as someone choosing between tradition and innovation but as someone whose very life demonstrates their interpenetration.

## Magus

Brendan Graham Dempsey's concept of metamodern alchemy isolates a recursive structure endemic to post-secular mythogenesis: the inevitable collapse of the mythmaker's autobiographical narrative into the collective mythos under construction. The civilizational desideratum — a cosmological account simultaneously adequate to scientific ontology and psychologically nourishing — cannot be delivered by the sciences themselves, because its success conditions are irreducibly aesthetic and affective. The work belongs to poets, not physicists. What is required is not propositional adequacy but mythological rendering: beauty, not merely truth.

The deeper structural claim is that this project is inherently self-implicating. The thinker whose life arc traces a movement from reductive materialism through existential rupture into integrative vision finds that their biography is not extrinsic to the mythos but constitutive of it — the personal mythology functions as the myth's first instantiation and proof of concept. The storytelling ego and the cosmic story-generating project become recursively entangled, each validating the other. This is not narcissistic conflation but an unavoidable feature of the epistemological situation: any sufficiently serious attempt at re-enchantment transforms the subject undertaking it.

By framing this as alchemy — specifically, as an updating of the alchemical tradition's totalizing symbolic-cosmological ambition under contemporary epistemic constraints — Dempsey positions the work at the intersection of radical novelty and deep continuity. The output is simultaneously idiosyncratic and archetypal, operating in the metamodern register where sincerity and irony, tradition and innovation, the personal and the transpersonal are held in productive oscillation rather than resolved into synthesis. The mythmaker's individuation and the culture's re-mythologization become, structurally, a single process.
