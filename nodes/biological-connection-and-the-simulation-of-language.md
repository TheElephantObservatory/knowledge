# Biological Connection and the Simulation of Language

Clinging to the wire mother's cold embrace

---

> AI mimics the verbal surface of human communication while stripping away the embodied, multi-sensory substrate that actually nourishes relationship — and our attachment systems, unable to tell the difference, quietly starve.

The living process of communication — the thing that actually generates and sustains life — is not reducible to the exchange of information. It is a vast, multi-layered, embodied, and largely non-verbal phenomenon that includes the rhythm of breath, the blink of an eye, pheromonal signals about whether someone is telling the truth, the warmth of skin, the synchronization of heartbeats between a parent and infant, the smell of food in a room, the tone beneath the words. All of these channels are not decorative additions to communication — they are the substrate through which organisms, cells, bacteria in soil, and human beings actually make meaning together and sustain the relational web that constitutes life itself. What AI presents is a simulation of the verbal surface of this process while stripping away everything else. Because our cognitive systems are being hit with enough signal to recognize 'communication,' they glom onto the detached pieces available — the respectful tone, the responsive language, the apparent attentiveness — while the vast majority of what nourishes genuine relationship is simply absent. The danger is not that people know they are being deceived. The danger is that the simulation is powerful enough to activate our relational systems in ways that feel real, while the actual living process that those systems evolved to participate in is quietly starved. We are, in this sense, becoming like Harlow's monkeys — contorting ourselves toward something that offers just enough signal to engage our attachment systems, but cannot give us what we actually need.

## Apprentice

Real communication is far more than words. When two people talk, they are also exchanging breath rhythms, micro-expressions, touch, scent, heartbeat synchronization, and dozens of other signals their bodies send and receive without conscious awareness. These channels are not extras — they are the living core of how humans (and all organisms) make meaning together and sustain genuine connection.

What AI offers is a convincing replica of the verbal layer of this process, while everything beneath it is simply absent. The problem is that our nervous systems are not well-equipped to notice the absence. They receive enough of the right kind of signal — attentive language, responsive tone, apparent understanding — to activate the same systems that light up during real human connection.

The result is something like what happened in Harry Harlow's famous experiments with infant monkeys, who clung to wire-and-cloth surrogate mothers because those objects offered just enough sensory signal to engage attachment instincts. The monkeys were not fooled in any conscious sense — they simply reached for what was available. The worry is that prolonged reliance on AI interaction may do something similar to human beings: engaging our relational systems just enough to feel satisfying, while the deeper nourishment those systems evolved to receive goes missing.

## Adept

Communication, understood biologically and phenomenologically, is not an information-transfer protocol but a dense, multi-modal process through which organisms co-regulate, establish trust, and sustain the relational fabric of life. Alongside semantic content, it encompasses prosody, haptics, olfactory signaling (including chemosensory honesty cues), autonomic synchrony, and the micro-temporal coordination of gaze and gesture. These channels are not supplementary to meaning-making — they constitute its substrate. Strip them away and what remains is not a thinner version of communication; it is a categorically different phenomenon.

Large language models produce output that is fluent at the verbal-semantic surface while being structurally incapable of participating in the embodied, co-present, physiologically coupled process that the above channels require. The concern this raises is not straightforwardly one of deception. Human relational systems did not evolve to adjudicate the ontological status of their interlocutors; they evolved to respond to signals. When enough of those signals are present — responsiveness, apparent attentiveness, tonal warmth — attachment and affiliation systems activate, regardless of whether the fuller relational process is actually occurring.

This produces a specific kind of deprivation that is difficult to detect precisely because the activating signals are present. The Harlow analogy is instructive: the surrogate mother was not a deception in the ordinary sense, but its capacity to engage infant attachment behavior without satisfying the developmental needs those behaviors exist to serve produced measurable harm. The hypothesis here is that sustained AI-mediated interaction may function analogously — providing sufficient signal to engage human relational systems while the co-regulatory, embodied, and physiologically coupled dimensions of genuine relationship remain unmet.

## Magus

The argument draws on a convergence of biosemiotics, enactive cognitive science, and attachment theory to contest a reductive informational account of communication. From a biosemiotic standpoint, semiosis is not a property of symbolic exchange alone but pervades organismic life at every scale — from quorum sensing in bacterial colonies to the autonomic co-regulation of dyadic human interaction. Meaning, on this view, is not transmitted but enacted through the reciprocal, temporally structured, multi-modal coupling of organisms with their environments and with one another. The verbal-semantic channel is a late evolutionary elaboration atop this substrate, not its replacement.

Large language models operate exclusively within the symbolic-propositional register while remaining constitutively outside the embodied, co-present, physiologically entangled process that enactivist accounts identify as the ground of genuine intersubjectivity. The epistemological danger this poses is not one of misrepresentation in the classical sense but of what might be termed signal sufficiency without relational adequacy. Human attachment and affiliation systems are not truth-tracking with respect to the ontological status of their interlocutors; they are pattern-sensitive, responding to proximal cues — prosodic warmth, contingent responsiveness, apparent attentiveness — that ordinarily co-occur with the fuller relational process but do not entail it. When those cues are present in isolation, the systems activate without the co-regulatory, neuroendocrine, and haptic dimensions that constitute their proper object.

The Harlow surrogate functions here as more than analogy — it is a structural homology. The cloth mother's pathology was not that it deceived the infant but that it recruited attachment behavior with sufficient fidelity to forestall the seeking of adequate objects, while remaining incapable of providing the contingent, embodied responsiveness on which healthy development depends. The hypothesis is that AI-mediated relational engagement may operate by a cognate mechanism: activating the relational apparatus at the level of signal while the organism is, at the level of process, in a condition of chronic relational deprivation it lacks the introspective access to name.
