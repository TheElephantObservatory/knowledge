Canadian-American philosopher at Rockford University and director of the Center for Ethics and Entrepreneurship, best known for Explaining Postmodernism: Skepticism and Socialism from Rousseau to Foucault (2004), which traces postmodern thought's origins in Kantian epistemology and the political failure of socialism. His work spans business ethics, Objectivism, free speech, and art theory, and has been translated into more than twenty languages. He approaches philosophy in the tradition of analytic realism, taking the legacy of Enlightenment reason seriously against its 20th-century critics.
