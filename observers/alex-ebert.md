Frontman and founder of Edward Sharpe and the Magnetic Zeros, the Los Angeles folk-rock collective known for their communal, spiritually charged ethos and the song Home. Beyond music, Ebert works as a philosopher and writer exploring consciousness, shadow work, and the meaning crisis through his Substack Bad Guru. He is also an award-winning film composer, having won a Golden Globe for his score to All Is Lost.
