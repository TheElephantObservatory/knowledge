Software entrepreneur and physicist who co-founded Anaconda (formerly Continuum Analytics), the distribution that made Python's scientific computing stack accessible to millions of researchers and engineers worldwide. A creator of the PyData conference community and co-founder of NumFOCUS, he has long championed open-source scientific computing as a public good. In recent years he has written on distributed internet architecture, the moral philosophy of digital systems, and what the web could have been.
