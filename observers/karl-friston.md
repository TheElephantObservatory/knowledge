Neuroscientist at University College London and one of the most highly cited scientists alive, Friston is best known for developing Statistical Parametric Mapping — the global standard for analyzing brain imaging data — and the free energy principle, a unified mathematical framework proposing that all adaptive biological systems minimize surprise by modeling their environment and acting to confirm predictions. His active inference framework has become foundational in computational psychiatry, cognitive science, and theoretical AI.
