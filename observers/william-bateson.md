William Bateson (1861–1926) was an English biologist who founded and named the science of genetics. After discovering Gregor Mendel's neglected work in 1900, he became its most vigorous champion in the English-speaking world — translating Mendel's paper and running landmark breeding experiments with Reginald Punnett, co-discovering genetic linkage. His 1894 book Materials for the Study of Variation was a foundational text of the new discipline, and he was the father of anthropologist and cybernetician Gregory Bateson.
