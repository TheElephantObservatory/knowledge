Ecologist and philosopher who has spent thirty years conducting fieldwork across Earth's major habitats, developing a framework called Oika — a living synthesis of complexity science, deep time, art, and ecological intelligence. His work reframes humans as continuous participants in the evolutionary process of the cosmos, arguing that reality is fundamentally relational and that intelligence extends far beyond individual brains. He is Scientist-in-Residence at the Maria Mitchell Association on Nantucket Island.
