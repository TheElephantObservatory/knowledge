Theoretical biologist and complexity scientist whose decades of work at the Santa Fe Institute challenged the neo-Darwinian orthodoxy by arguing that self-organization is as fundamental to life as natural selection. Best known for his theories on autocatalytic sets and random Boolean networks — elaborated in The Origins of Order (1993) and At Home in the Universe — Kauffman proposes that life emerges spontaneously at "the edge of chaos" as a natural property of sufficiently complex chemical networks.
