Aboriginal scholar, author, and Senior Research Fellow at Deakin University, where he founded the Indigenous Knowledge Systems Lab. His landmark book Sand Talk: How Indigenous Thinking Can Save the World uses Aboriginal frameworks — pattern, kinship, story, and deep-time relationality — to critique Western linear thinking on sustainability, governance, and systems design. His follow-up Right Story, Wrong Story (2023) continues to explore how Indigenous ways of knowing can offer systemic alternatives to extractive and computational worldviews.
