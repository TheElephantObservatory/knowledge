Gregory Bateson (1904–1980) was a British-American anthropologist, social scientist, and cybernetician whose work permanently altered how we think about mind, ecology, and pattern. His collected essays Steps to an Ecology of Mind (1972) range across anthropology, psychiatry, cybernetics, and epistemology, introducing concepts such as the double bind, logical types, and the ecology of ideas. A foundational figure in systems thinking, he argued that mind is not located in individual brains but is immanent in the larger systems — biological, social, ecological — in which organisms are embedded.
