Canadian-British science fiction author and digital rights activist whose novels — including Little Brother, Walkaway, and The Internet Con — blend speculative fiction with sharp critiques of surveillance capitalism, copyright maximalism, and platform monopolies. He coined the term 'enshittification' to describe the self-destructive dynamics of dominant internet platforms, and is a longtime special advisor to the Electronic Frontier Foundation. He publishes daily commentary at Pluralistic.net and advocates for interoperability, antitrust enforcement, and an open web.
