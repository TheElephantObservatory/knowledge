Independent scholar and public intellectual working at the intersection of philosophy of religion, Islamic studies, and metamodern spirituality. He is the founder and director of Alif: Traditional Wisdom in Review, a publication bridging classical Islamic thought and Western intellectual traditions, and works with the Center for Process Studies. His scholarship addresses nihilism, interreligious conflict, and the contemporary meaning crisis through process philosophy and comparative theology.
