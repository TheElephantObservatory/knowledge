American professor emeritus of communication and rhetoric at the University of Memphis, known for his scholarship on the rhetoric of science — particularly Darwin's theory of evolution and its public reception. He edited Darwinism, Design and Public Education (2003) with Stephen Meyer and was named an expert witness for the defense in the landmark Kitzmiller v. Dover Area School District case on intelligent design in public schools. A fellow of the Discovery Institute's Center for Science and Culture, his work explores how scientific ideas are communicated, contested, and legitimized in public discourse.
