Social-cognitive psychologist at the University of Virginia and co-founder and executive director of the Center for Open Science, dedicated to improving transparency and reproducibility across scientific disciplines. He led the landmark 2011 Reproducibility Project, which found that only 36% of 100 psychology studies replicated successfully, triggering a discipline-wide reckoning with research practices. He also co-developed the Implicit Association Test, a foundational tool for measuring implicit bias.
