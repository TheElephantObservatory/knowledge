Evolutionary biologist and complexity scientist serving as President and William H. Miller Professor of Complex Systems at the Santa Fe Institute, the world's leading research center for complex adaptive systems. His work investigates the co-evolution of intelligence, language, and information — from the origin of life and the emergence of cognition to the ways humans externalize and amplify intelligence through artifacts, institutions, and technology. He is a leading public voice on how complexity science illuminates the deepest questions about life, mind, and civilization.
