Media theorist, professor at CUNY Queens College, and author of more than 25 books, Rushkoff coined concepts like "viral media" and "digital native" before becoming one of technology's most incisive critics. Works including Program or Be Programmed, Throwing Rocks at the Google Bus, and Team Human argue that digital capitalism subordinates human agency to extractive corporate logic, and that recovering genuine human connection requires treating people — not growth — as the operating system's goal.
