John Krakauer is the John C. Malone Professor of Neurology, Neuroscience, and Physical Medicine & Rehabilitation at Johns Hopkins School of Medicine, where he directs the Center for the Study of Motor Learning and Brain Repair. His research integrates psychophysics, computational modeling, and brain imaging to understand human sensorimotor learning and recovery after stroke — exploring the relationship between motor control, cognitive architecture, and the nature of skill. He is External Faculty at the Santa Fe Institute and Chief Medical Director at MindMaze.
